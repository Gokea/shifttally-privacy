Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 4, 2026

At ShiftTally, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we handle your data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your personal data.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data You Provide

ShiftTally is designed to keep your data private and under your control. The following information is stored locally on your device:

  • Shift Information: Shift dates, start and end times, break durations, and notes
  • Earnings Data: Hourly rates, payment amounts, tips, and bonuses
  • Job Details: Job titles, employer names, and work locations
  • UK Postcodes: Location data when you use the postcode lookup feature
  • Payment Records: Payment status, payment dates, and payment methods
  • Custom Settings: App preferences, currency settings, and notification preferences

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

  • Device Information: iOS version, device model, and app version (for compatibility and support purposes)
  • Usage Analytics: Anonymous usage statistics to improve app functionality and performance
  • Crash Reports: Technical data when the app crashes to help us fix bugs

1.3 Third-Party Services

ShiftTally integrates with the following third-party services:

  • AdMob (Google): Displays advertisements in the free version of the app. AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data for ad personalization. You can opt out of personalized ads in your device settings.
  • Apple In-App Purchases: Processes premium feature purchases through Apple's secure payment system. We do not have access to your payment information.
  • UK Postcode Lookup API: When you use the postcode lookup feature, your postcode query is sent to our third-party API provider to retrieve location information.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

2.1 Core App Functionality

  • Track your work shifts and calculate earnings
  • Display payment history and analytics
  • Provide iOS widgets with your shift summaries
  • Enable export and backup features
  • Send you notifications about upcoming shifts (if enabled)

2.2 App Improvement

  • Analyze anonymous usage patterns to enhance user experience
  • Identify and fix technical issues and bugs
  • Develop new features based on user needs

2.3 Advertising (Free Tier Only)

  • Display relevant advertisements through Google AdMob
  • Measure ad performance and effectiveness

3. Data Storage and Security

3.1 Local Storage

Your shift data, earnings, and personal information are stored locally on your iOS device using secure, encrypted storage provided by Apple's iOS framework. This data remains on your device and is protected by your device's security features, including:

  • iOS encryption at rest
  • Face ID / Touch ID / Passcode protection
  • Secure iOS sandboxing

3.2 iCloud Backup (Optional)

If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device, your ShiftTally data may be included in your iCloud backups. This is managed by Apple, and we do not have access to your iCloud data. You can disable iCloud Backup for ShiftTally in your iOS Settings.

3.3 Export and Backup

When you use the export feature, your data is saved in formats you choose (CSV, PDF, JSON) and stored in locations you specify (Files app, email, other apps). You have complete control over where this exported data goes.

3.4 Data Retention

Your data remains on your device for as long as you keep the app installed. When you delete the app, all locally stored data is permanently removed from your device. Backed-up data in iCloud follows Apple's retention policies.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We only share data in the following limited circumstances:

4.1 Third-Party Service Providers

  • Google AdMob: Receives anonymous device identifiers and usage data for ad delivery (free tier only)
  • UK Postcode API: Receives postcode queries when you use the lookup feature
  • Apple: Processes in-app purchases through Apple's payment system

4.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or governmental regulation, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to:

  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Protect our rights and property
  • Prevent fraud or security issues
  • Protect user safety

4.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred to the new entity. We will notify you via email or app notification before your data is transferred.

5. Your Rights and Choices

You have complete control over your data in ShiftTally:

5.1 Access and Control

  • View Your Data: Access all your shift data, earnings, and settings within the app
  • Edit Your Data: Modify or correct any information at any time
  • Delete Your Data: Delete individual shifts or clear all data from the app settings
  • Export Your Data: Download your data in CSV, PDF, or JSON format

5.2 Advertising Choices

  • Opt Out of Personalized Ads: Disable ad personalization in iOS Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Limit Ad Tracking
  • Remove Ads Completely: Purchase the Premium version to eliminate all advertisements

5.3 Notification Control

  • Enable or disable shift notifications in the app settings
  • Manage notification permissions in iOS Settings

5.4 Widget Privacy

  • Choose what information appears in your iOS widgets
  • Remove widgets at any time from your home screen

6. GDPR Compliance (European Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

6.1 Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data based on:

  • Your Consent: When you agree to use our services and accept this privacy policy
  • Contractual Necessity: To provide the core functionality of the app
  • Legitimate Interest: To improve the app, prevent fraud, and ensure security

6.2 Your GDPR Rights

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of all data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Restriction: Limit how we process your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: File a complaint with your local data protection authority

6.3 Data Transfers

Since your data is stored locally on your device, international data transfers are minimal. When using third-party services (AdMob, UK Postcode API), data may be transferred to countries outside the EEA. These services use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses.
